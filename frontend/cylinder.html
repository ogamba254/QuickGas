Cylinder Handling & Storage

Proper handling of LPG cylinders is essential for your home's safety. Follow these industry-standard guidelines.

✅ The "DO"s

  • Keep Upright: Always store and use cylinders in an upright position to ensure only gas (not liquid) reaches the burner.
  • Ventilation: Store cylinders in well-ventilated areas, ideally at ground level.
  • Check Seals: Ensure the safety seal is intact upon delivery from QuickGas.
  • Soapy Water Test: Use soapy water to check for leaks at the valve connection—never use a flame.

❌ The "DON'T"s

  • No Heat: Never store cylinders near heat sources, electrical sockets, or flammable materials.
  • Don't Drop: Avoid dropping or sliding cylinders, as this can damage the valve or the base ring.
  • No Basement Storage: LPG is heavier than air; do not store cylinders in basements or cellars where gas can pool if a leak occurs.
  • Don't Force: Never force a regulator onto a cylinder. If it doesn't fit, call us.

How to Change a Cylinder Safely

  1. Extinguish all flames and turn off your cooker.
  2. Ensure the regulator is in the "OFF" position.
  3. Disconnect the regulator from the empty cylinder.
  4. Remove the safety seal from the new QuickGas cylinder.
  5. Check that the rubber 'O' ring inside the valve is present and not damaged.
  6. Attach the regulator firmly and switch it to "ON".
