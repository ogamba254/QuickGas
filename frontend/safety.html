Gas Safety First

Your safety is our priority. Please follow these guidelines for home gas usage.

What You Should Do

  • Keep the cylinder in an upright position at all times.
  • Ensure the kitchen is well-ventilated when cooking.
  • Check the rubber "O-ring" inside the valve for cracks.
  • Close the regulator switch when the stove is not in use.
  • Use soapy water to check for leaks (look for bubbles).

What You Should Avoid

  • Never use a flame (matchstick/lighter) to check for leaks.
  • Do not store cylinders in basements or unventilated cupboards.
  • Don't keep flammable materials (curtains, oil) near the stove.
  • Never try to repair a dented or leaking cylinder yourself.
  • Do not leave your cooking unattended for long periods.

In Case of a Gas Leak

If you smell gas, do not flip any electrical switches. Extinguish all open flames, open all windows, and move the cylinder outside if safe to do so.
